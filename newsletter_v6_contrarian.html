Everyone Saw a Top. I Saw a Flush.

The index fell hard and the average stock barely moved. That is not how tops begin.
Week of 2026-06-05
The ThesisFriday looked scary on the index, but the selling was surgical. It hit the crowded mega-cap and AI names while the broad field held. Concentration unwinding is healthy, not fatal. I am buying the dip in the parts of the market that do not need the Fed.

The headlines screamed a 4% Nasdaq day and the worst session in a year. The crowd is calling a top. I think they are reading the index, not the market. The S&P fell 2.6% but the median stock barely moved, and more than half of all stocks held above their 50-day line. A genuine top broadens; this one narrowed the damage to the exact names everyone already owned.
