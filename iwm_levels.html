At 289 the Russell sits right on its month value-area high and a stack of pivots clustered at 290, where the call wall also rests. The problem underneath is that price is floating roughly twenty-eight points above the one-year volume magnet at 261, the level where the most real business got done over the past year. Markets dislike trading far from their high-volume node for long, and the pull back toward it is gravitational rather than urgent. The levels that matter on the downside come quickly. Below that the picture gets interesting, because the 279 to 289 zone IWM is sitting in is a no-supply gap, meaning little volume was transacted there. The second-order point: the call wall at 290 caps upside while the put wall sits all the way down at 270, so dealers have left the floor twenty points lower than the ceiling. That asymmetry favors sharp air-pocket moves down over grinding melt-ups.
