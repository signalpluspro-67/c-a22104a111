It All Comes Down to 160

The yen broke its line the same day stocks cracked. That is not a coincidence.
Week of 2026-06-05
The ThesisStrip away the noise and one chart decides the next month. USDJPY just pushed through 160, the level that invites Tokyo to act and can force the carry trade to unwind. Friday's selloff and that break are the same event.

I can give you ten data points this week, but only one keeps me up at night. USDJPY closed Friday at 160.2. It broke the line I have written about in every letter for a month. Japan spent over 70 billion dollars defending the yen in April and May, and the market just erased all of it. The same jobs report that hit stocks widened the rate gap and pushed the yen straight through the level that invites intervention.

🧠 My Take

My one conviction: size to the yen, not to the headlines. I hold heavy cash, I keep risk in rate-insensitive names, and I treat a break back under 155 as the moment the carry regime turns live. Everything else is downstream of that one chart.
