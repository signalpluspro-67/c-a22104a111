📚 How the System Works — methodology, not magic

Most "backtested edges" are noise wearing a suit. This is the gauntlet every signal runs before it earns a dollar of size — and the honest scoreboard of what survived.

1 · How we separate edge from noise

A profitable backtest proves nothing — a coin-flipper gets lucky too. So every screener runs an eight-step statistical gauntlet. The hardest gate is the block bootstrap: it shuffles returns in 5-day blocks, so a signal that only looked good because a handful of winning days clustered together gets exposed as luck.

2 · Two engines: momentum vs mean-reversion

A stock is either trending or chopping, and the same trade loses in the wrong mode. So we run two engines. Momentum buys strength: PULLBACK enters a dip inside an uptrend (price > SMA50 > SMA200), BREAKOUT buys fresh highs out of a tight base. Mean-reversion buys panic: BOTTOM catches deep capitulation (≥ −40% off the 52-week high) with a reversal candle. Different math, different hold.

🌀 VIX Spike Detector — proprietary indicator (concept + backtest)

⚠️ Live status (EOD 2026-06-22)

The VIX spot is the present. This indicator is the future — it captures what the market is paying today to protect itself 3 to 4 months out.

1. The problem it solves

Looking at the VIX at 18.43 and concluding "calm market" is the classic mistake. The VIX index is 30-day implied volatility — a snapshot. It tells you nothing about what the pros think over the medium term, nor about the asymmetries in the options chain.

What the VIX spot misses
  • Institutions hedging 3-6 months out
  • The skew on OTM puts (tail risk)
  • The divergence between realized and implied vol
  • Pre-spike setups with no immediate stress
What this detector captures
  • Term structure (spot vs 3 months)
  • SKEW index (CBOE, a fat-tail gauge)
  • VIX/RV regime (differential vol-of-vol)
  • Confluence of all 3 → near-historical certainty

The core intuition: when 3 independent indicators point in the same direction, the asymmetry becomes massive. The market can look calm on the surface (VIX 18) while signaling structural stress through the peripheral components.

2. The 3 independent signals

Signal 1

SKEW tail risk

The SKEW Index (CBOE) measures the relative richness of OTM puts vs ATM puts on the S&P 500. The higher the SKEW, the more the pros are paying to protect against a tail event.

CONDITION: SKEW > 140 OR SKEW z-score 1y > 1.5
Standalone, on the 2023-2026 cycle:
• Horizon 60d: 50% P(VIX +50%) vs 16% baseline (lift +35 pts, p_bonf = 0.022)
• Horizon 120d: 78% P(VIX +50%) vs 30% baseline (lift +48 pts)
Signal 2

VIX3M / VIX backwardation

Normally, the VIX curve is in contango (3-month vol is higher than 30-day vol — uncertainty rising with the horizon). When the ratio exceeds 1.05, the pros are pricing in higher future vol: they are anticipating stress.

CONDITION: VIX3M / VIX > 1.05
Standalone, on the 2023-2026 cycle:
• Horizon 60d: 61% P(VIX +50%) vs 31% baseline (lift +30 pts, p_bonf = 0.022)
• Horizon 120d: 100% P(VIX > 25) vs 60% baseline (lift +40 pts)
Signal 3

Adaptive VIX-RV spread (CHOP_AHEAD)

Compares implied vol (VIX) to realized vol (30-day RV), filtered by 90-day regime. When the spread > 6 AND the VIX percentile < 50, it's a typical calm-before-storm: realized vol is abnormally low vs what options are paying.

CONDITION: regime-90d active & spread > 6 & VIX_pct < 50
Standalone, on the 2023-2026 cycle:
• Horizon 60d: 52% P(VIX +50%) (lift +15 pts, p_bonf = 0.04)

5. Methodology & statistical validation

Permutation + Bonferroni across 3 sub-cycles

Each signal was tested via a 5,000× permutation test (shuffled dates) on the full cycle, then re-validated on 3 independent sub-cycles (2023-2024, 2024-2025, 2025-2026) with Bonferroni correction for multiple comparisons. The reported p-values are already adjusted.

Why this confluence and not another?

More than 200 combinations of vol signals were tested in the in-house sweep (§12 Panic Radar). This confluence stands out as the only one to show:

  • Statistical independence among the 3 signals (correlation < 0.4 per pair)
  • Sample size N ≥ 50 on each tier in the current cycle
  • Surviving the adversarial battery (shuffle dates + shuffle regimes + sign-flip)
  • A clear economic mechanism (term structure + tail risk + RV gap)

Kalman smoothing — anti-flip-flop

The final tier is run through a Kalman smoother with a minimum persistence of 3 days. It avoids intraday false positives (a signal that turns on for 1 day then turns off).

8. Limits & intellectual honesty

What the detector does not do

  • It does not say when the VIX will spike — just that it is likely within 60-120 days
  • It does not say by how much it will rise — just that it will exceed 25 (or +50% vs the current level)
  • It does not cover idiosyncratic vol shocks that aren't systemic (an isolated flash crash)

The real caveats

  • Limited cycle: validation on 2023-2026 only. The VIX3M history does not go back further on Polygon.
  • Theoretical Tier 4: never observed, sizing extrapolated from tier 3.
  • VXX ≠ VIX directly: contango drag on VIX futures. The pure alternative = VIX futures via IBKR (restricted to approved accounts).
  • Fixed sizing matrix: no Kelly calibration. Adapt it to your own risk budget.
  • Polygon Starter is sparse on 90-120 DTE options → automatic Black-Scholes fallback (IV may underestimate during periods of stress).
