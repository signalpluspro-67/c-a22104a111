Newsletter-ready (copy & paste, Citrini style)

The setup: QQQ is pinned at the ceiling of its recent value, and the air both above and below it is thin. Price at 727 sits right on a shelf of resistance that stacks tightly between 725 and 729, where the month value-area high, the 60-day value-area high, and the first two pivots all converge. That is a lot of overhead memory in a two-point band, and it explains why the tape keeps stalling here rather than tearing higher. The real magnet above is the 750 call wall, but nothing structural fills the space until price either clears 729 with conviction or gives it back. The more interesting feature is what lies beneath. The bulk of this year's trading volume rests far lower, with the month point of control at 705, the 60-day node at 602, and the one-year volume magnet at 600. Between 628 and 725 there is a no-supply gap, meaning very little business was transacted on the way up. Price is currently perched at the very top of that vacuum. Gaps like this cut both ways: they let rallies extend quickly because there is no seller memory to absorb them, but they also offer no support if sentiment turns, which is why a slip back through 725 could see 705 tested faster than the calm chart suggests. The second-order point is that low volume at a high is fragile by construction. Acceptance, not a single green day, is what would confirm the breakout. What would change the view: daily closes holding above 729 and building volume into the 730s, turning today's ceiling into a floor. Not financial advice.
