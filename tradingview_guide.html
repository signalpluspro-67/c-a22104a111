What it shows you

A simple two-filter regime gate. The daily EMA 200 says the trend; the weekly EMA 50 confirms the bigger picture. Both green = the backtested long environment. The BUY markers on the chart show each fresh entry.

How to read it

GO LONG = price above EMA 200 and weekly close above weekly EMA 50. WAIT = at least one is off, stay in cash. Bars since last BUY tells you how fresh the last fresh cross was.

When to act

The edge is a fresh daily cross above EMA 200 while the weekly filter is already green. A small bars since BUY number = the signal just fired. A big number = you are mid-trend, not at a fresh entry.

What it shows you

Where the real trading happened. The volume profile finds the price with the most volume (VPOC) and the band that held ~70% of it (VAH to VAL) for each recent month. Current month VPOC 732, value area 732–753. On the chart those are the labelled horizontal lines — they act as magnets and as support/resistance.

How to read it

The dashboard gives you the one-glance read: Bias (above or below the month VPOC), the nearest support and resistance with the distance to each, where price sits in the range (the bar), and the resulting long R:R. Green R:R (≥ 2:1) = room to the upside.

When to act

Buy nearer support (low in the range, price near VAL) with resistance far above (good R:R). Avoid longs pinned under resistance (high in the range, poor R:R). A clean break and hold above VAH flips the bias bullish; losing VAL flips it bearish.
