Z-score is the current spread versus its 100-day mean (in standard deviations). Honesty: trading the spread mean-reversion is REJECT out of sample (cointegration broke after 2022). Read the extremes as relative-strength context, and use the hedge ratio for sizing a market-neutral leg, not as a standalone trade.
How to read
Hedge ratio = OLS slope: how many shares of leg B offset one share of leg A. A z-score past +2 means A is rich versus B (and vice versa). Correlation gauges whether the pair still moves together (low correlation = the relationship has decayed).

For a long position, short beta times the notional in SPY to neutralize market risk. Example column assumes a $10,000 long. Beta is the 1-year daily regression versus SPY. This is the part of the pairs toolkit that holds up: a clean market hedge, independent of any mean-reversion claim.
How to read
Beta measures how much the name moves per 1% SPY move. Short beta x notional of SPY to hedge the market component, leaving the stock-specific bet. Re-check beta as it drifts.
