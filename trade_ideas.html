XYZ: orderly pullback into the EMA cluster, buyers showing up

Signal

Trend pullback to support with bullish EMA stack. Score: 78/100.

The Setup

XYZ is a mid-cap name working a clean uptrend after basing earlier this year.

The price story is straightforward. The 52-week range runs from $49.09 at the low to $81.11 at the high. After tagging the swing high near $76.10, the stock pulled back to a swing low at $66.63 and now trades at $73.25.

The EMA picture is constructive. The blue EMA (20) sits at $71.16, the red EMA (50) sits at $69.46, and price is above both. Blue above red is the bullish alignment you want before committing capital.

Why It Flagged

I run 3,000+ US stocks through proprietary models every day, and this one fits the trend-pullback profile cleanly.

The models look for three things stacked together: an intact uptrend, an orderly pullback that does not break structure, and buyers stepping in at a level you can defend. XYZ checks each box. The pullback held above the swing low at $66.63, which lines up with the nearest support at $67.02. Price reclaimed the 20-EMA at $71.16 instead of bleeding through it. The next horizontal resistance sits at $82.60, which gives the trade room to work.

The pullback was shallow, the bounce was prompt, and the EMA stack stayed bullish. That is the textbook shape (yes, I will allow myself that word once) the models want to see.

The Trade

Entry: $73.25

Stop: $67.02, which sits just below the swing low at $66.63 and the 50-EMA at $69.46. If price closes below that zone, the trend thesis is broken.

Target: $82.60, the prior resistance and just above the 52-week high.

Reward to risk: 1.5:1.

Position Sizing

Entry to stop distance is 8.5%, which is wide. That is the cost of using structure for the stop instead of a tight, noise-driven level.

For a $100,000 account:

  • 1% account risk = $1,000 at risk, position size around $11,800 of XYZ.
  • 0.5% account risk = $500 at risk, position size around $5,900.

Cap the position at 10% of the book regardless of the math. Wide stops tempt you into oversized positions, do not let them.

What Could Go Wrong

The stop is 8.5% away. That is a real bite if it triggers, and a sloppy intraday flush can take you out before the daily close confirms anything. Plan for that.

The macro calendar is busy. FOMC lands today, Quad Witching hits in 2 days, and Russell rebalance is 9 days out. Single-name technicals can get steamrolled by index flows and volatility around these events. Size accordingly.

If price loses $69.46 (the 50-EMA) on a closing basis before it loses $67.02, the structure is degrading even if the hard stop has not hit. That is your soft warning.

My Take

This is a clean setup on the chart, with a score of 78/100 and an EMA stack doing the right things. The reward to risk at 1.5:1 is acceptable but not generous, so the trade earns its keep on execution and sizing, not on a fat payoff. I would take a starter at $73.25 and let the macro week filter the noise before adding. If buyers defend $71.16 through FOMC and Quad Witching, you have a runway to $82.60.
