Four months ago I started building something, and today I am handing it to you.

For two years I have written this newsletter the hard way: every number pulled by hand, name by name, late into the night. It worked, but one person can only read so many charts before the open. So I built the machine I always wanted, the one that does the grunt work while I sleep.

I did not do it alone. Along the way I asked the people who read me, the ones who know how I think and how I trade, what they actually needed. You told me what you use, what you skip, where you get stuck. This app is shaped around those answers. That is why I keep calling it ours.

Let me walk you through it, tab by tab, the way I use it every morning.

Today. I open this first. It is the morning glance: what moved overnight, the names I follow, the earnings due this week, and the state of the market underneath it all. Thirty seconds and I know the weather before I place a single trade.

[ IMAGE: Today ]

Screeners. This is the core. Every night after the close, the engine reads the whole US market, 12,000+ names, and runs a dozen tested setups on it: the dip buys, the breakouts, the momentum leaders, the squeezes, the unusual options flow, the names quietly bottoming out. What fits comes out ranked, each with a conviction score and a plain read of why it is there. Open any name from the board and the whole story lands on one screen: a setup score and a fundamentals score out of 100, the levels including the price where the idea is wrong, and where the money is moving, the named funds adding or trimming (13F, an inference), the options premium, the insider prints.

[ IMAGE: Screeners ]

Sectors. This shows me where the money is flowing, which corners of the market are leading and which are bleeding. It tells me whether the name I like is swimming with the current or against it, before I size anything.

[ IMAGE: Sectors ]

Themes. The market moves by story. Themes reads the narratives actually pulling capital right now and the names riding each one. It is how I catch a move while it is still a theme and before it becomes a headline everyone is chasing.

[ IMAGE: Themes ]

Socials. This shows me what the crowd is doing on a name, where the retail attention and the chatter are pointing. Sometimes it is the early tell. Sometimes it is the reason to fade. Either way I want to see it before I act.

[ IMAGE: Socials ]

Positions. This is where I build and manage the book. Paste what you hold and get a read on every line: trim, hold, add or cut. A calculator sizes each new trade to your account and sets the stop. A watchlist alerts you when your names hit their zone, so they come to you instead of you refreshing charts all day. The decision gets made with a clear head, and the journal keeps it, so the next trade starts smarter than the last.

[ IMAGE: Positions ]

One honest word. This is a tool to think with, and it will not print money on its own. The edge is tested across years of market history, and it still misses on plenty of names. That is why every read leads with where it is wrong. You bring the judgment. The app brings the work.

And it is only the start. I ship improvements every week, and members get each one as it lands. I want to take this a lot further, in exactly this direction, with you.

Nothing changes with the newsletter. I keep writing every issue myself, same as before. The app is the engine underneath, there for you between issues.
