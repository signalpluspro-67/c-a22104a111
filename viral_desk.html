Polymath 1
XLK led at 1.7%. XLV lagged at -1.1%. Staples and Utilities down a full point on a green day.

The consensus read is a healthy risk-on rotation. What's actually happening is defensives getting sold on an up tape, which is a different animal. When Staples and Utilities bleed while the index prints new highs, the market is not just buying growth. It's actively liquidating the hiding spots. That is a late-cycle behavior dressed up as a bullish one.

Crowds sell shelter last, and only when they've stopped believing they'll need it.

Polymath 2
SPY +0.9%. VIX at 15.6.

Vol under 16 with the index adding almost a percent is the kind of tape that trains people to buy every dip mechanically. The consensus read is that low vol is confirmation. The better read is that low vol is the fee you pay to own the next surprise. Realized vol this compressed against a broad advance sets up an environment where the reflex trade works until the day it stops working, and by then the reflex is the position.

The market never warns twice, and it never charges you at the same price it did yesterday.
