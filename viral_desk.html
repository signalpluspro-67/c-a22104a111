Polymath 2
VIX at 15.6 while QQQ prints up 1.4 percent. Volatility does not usually get compressed on a green tech day and still leave anything for the buyers left standing.

The consensus wants low vol to mean complacency. It can also mean the reflexive bid stopped paying up for hedges. Two different worlds, one same number.

The map is not the territory. VIX prints a level. What matters is which market is standing around it, and today the market standing around it is one that stopped being scared.

📝 Essay notes · 53 Funds Built to Beat the Market. Almost None Beat Doing Nothing.

Idea minimalism from the latest essay: hook, building blocks, payload. Restack bait, zero product.
Note 1
Someone lined up 53 hedge fund products against just owning SPY.
More than 40 of them lost.
Most sophisticated products end up worse than the plain thing.
Note 2
Every fund with a stellar recent number in that table has three or four months of history.
Every one with a full track record sits in the middle of the pack.
Recency wears a lab coat.
Note 3
BTAL was built to rise when the market falls.
It bled capital for six years while the tape climbed.
Crash insurance you pay for every day gets expensive fast.
Note 4
Low downside capture is worthless if you kill your upside with it.
Most hedges do exactly that.
Up years pay for the down ones. Give those up and you die slowly.
Note 5
The cleanest outperformer on the whole list was SPMO.
An S&P momentum ETF that barely hedges anything.
It owns the index with a tilt. That is the whole trick.
Note 6
The window in that study was end of 2019 through mid 2026.
One of the great equity runs ever.
Judging crash insurance during a melt-up is the wrong test.
But if a fund cannot survive the up years, nobody owns it when the down year finally comes.
