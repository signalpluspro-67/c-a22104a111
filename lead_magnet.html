How I Read the Market in One Page

Most market writing tells you what happened. You can get that anywhere, for free, ten minutes after the close. What is harder to find is a way to read the tape that tells you what happens next, and gives you one thing to actually do about it.

This is the method behind every issue of The Philosopher Investor. It fits on one page. Steal it.

1. Start with the regime, not the headline

Before any single stock, I ask one question: what kind of market is this? Risk-on or risk-off, broadening or narrowing, calm or coiled. The regime decides which playbook even works this week. A breakout in a broadening tape is a gift. The same breakout in a thinning tape is a trap. Same chart, opposite trade, and the regime tells you which one.

2. Read the internals, not the index

The index can print a new high while most stocks quietly roll over. That gap, between what the headline number says and what the average stock is doing, is where the real information lives. When participation thins under a rising index, the rally is running on fewer and fewer names. That is the classic late-stage tell, and it is invisible if you only watch the S&P print.

3. Follow where the money is going

Money is always rotating, out of one sector and into another. The job is to notice the move while it is early, before it is a headline. I watch which sectors are gaining relative strength and which are bleeding it, then ask the only question that matters: what is the catalyst, and is it just starting or nearly over.

4. Express it as one trade with a written invalidation

A view you cannot act on is a diary entry. So every week ends with one complete trade: a name, a buy zone, a stop, a target, and one plain sentence that says what would prove me wrong. I grade each setup on a simple public framework, E.D.G.E.:

  • E (Evidence): how many independent reads agree.
  • D (Direction): does the macro regime allow this trade.
  • G (Gain vs risk): reward against risk at the stated levels.
  • E (Exit): a stop and a written invalidation, every time.

No tip is worth anything without the level that kills it. The invalidation is the trade. The rest is commentary.
