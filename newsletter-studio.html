This is the dangerous part: when seven stocks carry an entire index, the index is mathematically fragile. If those seven names give back even a third of their move, the SPY drops -5% to -8% mechanically. No catalyst required. No macro change. Just a normal rebalance in a top-heavy structure.

The pullback we are seeing right now — three red sessions and -1.2% off the ATH at $748.17 — is exactly what that mechanic looks like when it starts.

VIX sits at 18.4. That is calm. By itself, the spot reading would tell you to stay long.

But the term structure is more interesting. VIX3M / VIX = 1.159. That means 3-month implied vol is trading 15% above spot. In calm markets, this is normal. Longer-dated vol typically sits above front-month vol.

The level I watch is closer to 1.30. When this ratio stretches toward 1.30, it means front-month is compressed while the curve is quietly pricing future risk. That kind of setup often shows up near market tops — calm today, but the curve is whispering tomorrow.

We are not there yet. At 1.159, the signal is mildly elevated, not extreme. So I would not use volatility as the main evidence for caution. The better takeaway:

Volatility is not flashing red, but it is not blocking the bear case either.

Tail protection is still reasonably priced. If you already have concerns from the three signals above, the vol market gives you room to hedge before protection gets expensive. [AJOUTE TON ANGLE OPTIONS — strikes, DTE, sizing préféré]
