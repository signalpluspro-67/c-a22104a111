The Tape Gave Back a Little. That's the Tell.

Wednesday, July 8 2026

Saturday I wrote that the rally had earned its stripes but was getting long in the tooth. Three days later the tape agrees. SPY closed at 743.65, down -0.5% on the day. QQQ slipped -0.3%. Small caps via IWM fell -0.5% to 294.75. None of this is dramatic. All of it is consistent with a market that's digesting, not rolling over.

The thesis is on track.

What the Pullback Tells You

VIX popped +6.1% to 17.11. That number matters because of where it came from. We spent the past two weeks watching volatility get compressed into a tight range below 16.5. A 17 handle isn't fear. It's the market waking up from a nap and checking the clock.

The move lower in equities was broad. Not concentrated in one sector, not driven by a single name gapping down on earnings. SPY, QQQ, and IWM all fell about the same amount. When everything drops together by half a percent, you're watching positioning adjust, not conviction change. Funds trimming into strength. Algos pulling bids a tick lower. The kind of move that shakes out the weakest hands and sets up entries for the next leg.

This is where patience earns its keep.

I've been watching the setups our desk flagged and two of them got more interesting with today's fade. CPB at $22.81 sits right on a buy zone with short squeeze confluence, a variance premium signal (meaning options are pricing in more volatility than the stock is delivering, which tends to resolve higher), and a bottom pattern all lining up. The stop is $21.13, the target is $25.34. That's about 11% of upside for 7% of risk. Not spectacular math, but the squeeze component adds a non-linear kicker if it triggers.

CI is the setup I find most compelling. Entry around $284.94, target $305.86, stop $271.00. You've got a moving average crossover confirming a bottom with a pullback entry. Healthcare names like CI tend to act as safe harbors when the broad market softens. If this tape stays choppy into Friday, money will rotate toward defensive pockets. CI is sitting right where that flow would land.

AVO at $13.51 is the smallest name of the three. Target $15.09, stop $12.45. The pattern is similar to CI: moving average cross plus bottom plus pullback. I like the structure but the liquidity is thinner, so position sizing matters more here. If you're going to take it, keep it small.

Three longs, all with bottom and pullback confluence. That's the desk telling you the same thing the tape is telling you: this is a buyable dip, not a trend change.

The Question Nobody's Asking

Everyone's focused on whether this pullback extends to -1% or -2%. Wrong question. The right question is what happens to VIX if the pullback doesn't extend.

If SPY holds above 740 through Friday and VIX drifts back below 16, you'll have a volatility compression setup on top of a price consolidation. That's the kind of coiled spring that produces fast moves higher. The crowd will have spent the week worrying about downside while the actual setup was building for upside.

I've seen this pattern enough times to respect it. The market pulls back 0.5% to 1% over two or three sessions. Financial media runs cautious headlines. Retail traders tighten stops. And then the market rips because the sellers exhausted themselves over a move that barely registered on a weekly chart.

That doesn't mean it has to play out that way. If VIX pushes through 18.5 or SPY breaks 735, the character changes. Then you're dealing with something more than a garden-variety pullback and the long setups need to be re-evaluated.

But we're not there. Not close.

What I'm Watching Into Friday

Two things.

First, SPY 740. That's the line where this pullback either stays routine or starts to mean something. Every session that closes above 740 reinforces the buyable-dip thesis. A close below it, especially on volume, and I'll start trimming risk.

Second, VIX 18.5. A one-day spike to 17 is noise. A grind toward 18.5 over multiple sessions is the market repricing risk, and that changes the playbook. Watch how it gets there as much as where it goes. A slow climb is worse than a spike, because spikes reverse and grinds tend to continue.

The setups are there. The thesis from Saturday holds. The tape gave back a little and told you exactly what kind of pullback this is: shallow, broad, and unaccompanied by panic. That's the kind you buy, not the kind you run from.

Stay patient. Let the levels do the talking.
