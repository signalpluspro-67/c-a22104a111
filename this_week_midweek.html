What the bounce tells us

The move since Saturday has been textbook post-flush behavior. Broad, fast, and led by the names that got hit hardest. QQQ up 1.4% on the day. IWM up 0.9%. This is not narrow megacap drag. Small caps participating in a relief rally means the bid is real, not cosmetic.

VIX collapsing from the low 20s to sub-19 in days is the market's way of saying the fear trade is over. For now. When vol compresses this fast after a spike, it tells you the hedges are coming off. Dealers who were short gamma are getting closer to neutral. That removes the mechanical selling pressure that made the flush feel worse than it was.

Here's where it gets interesting. SPY 742 puts us right at the level where selling accelerated three weeks ago. This is the zone that broke. Now we're testing it from below. Old support, new resistance. The market has to prove it can hold here, not on a single green day, but through a full session of selling pressure without giving back the move.

I've seen this pattern enough times. The flush clears the weak hands. The snap back feels euphoric. Then the real test comes: can the buyers who stepped in at the lows defend their positions when the first piece of bad news hits? That answer comes this week and next.

The part nobody wants to hear

The flush thesis working does not mean we're clear. SPY reclaiming 742 is step one. Step two is holding above 735 on any retest. If we break back below that level on volume, the flush wasn't a flush. It was a pause in something larger.

I don't think that's the base case. The credit market isn't confirming stress. HY spreads are stable. Breadth improved this week. But I've been wrong before, and the market doesn't care about my base case.

Position sizing matters more than direction here. The names I listed above all have defined stops. Use them. A flush that finds a floor can still chop for weeks before resolving higher. You don't need to be a hero with size. You need to be right on direction and patient on timing.
