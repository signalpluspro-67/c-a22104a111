The Flush Worked. Now Comes the Harder Part.

Monday, June 8 2026

Saturday's thesis is intact. The crowd called a top. The tape flushed weak hands and reversed. SPY sits at 742 on Wednesday evening, VIX got crushed to 18.97, down nearly 12% on the day alone. That's not a market rolling over. That's a market that shook the tree, found buyers, and kept walking.

The move since Saturday tells you something specific. When everyone leans bearish at the same time, the first move is often in their direction. That's the flush. It feels validating for about 48 hours. Then the market does what markets do to crowded trades: it punishes them. The VIX collapsing from the low 20s to sub-19 in a few sessions is the tell. Vol sellers stepped in hard. The put buyers from last week are watching premium decay eat their positions alive.

Look at what's leading. QQQ up 1.4% on the day, outpacing SPY and IWM. That's not defensive rotation. That's risk appetite returning to the names with the highest beta to sentiment. Small caps joining at +0.9% confirms it's broad. This isn't three mega-caps dragging an index higher while everything else bleeds. The bid is wide.

Now for the harder part.

A flush that works doesn't mean you chase. The easy money in this move was buying the fear. That window is closing. SPY at 742 is approaching the zone where the next question gets uncomfortable: is this a bounce, or a continuation? I don't have that answer yet. Nobody does on a Wednesday. But I know what to watch.

Our desk flagged three setups this week that fit the thesis. CI at $287, FRME at $40.27, SHC at $15.66. All three share the same confluence pattern: MA_CROSS plus PULLBACK plus BOTTOM firing together. That triple stack doesn't happen often. When it does, it means the name pulled back into a support zone, found a floor, and the moving averages are now crossing in the direction of the trade. It's the market's way of saying: this name got cheap for a reason that's no longer valid.

CI is the one I find most interesting. Entry at $287 with a target of $308 and a stop at $272. That's a reward-to-risk of about 1.5:1. Not spectacular, but the confluence score is what matters here. Three independent systems agreeing on the same name at the same time. The stop is wide enough to survive noise, tight enough to mean something if it breaks. If CI can't hold $272, the thesis is wrong and you move on.

FRME is tighter. $40.27 entry, $42.72 target, $38.64 stop. Smaller name, smaller range, similar setup. The trade works if the broad market cooperates. That's the catch with all three: they need SPY to hold this week's gains into the end of the week. If the flush reversal was real, these names follow. If SPY rolls back below 735, these stops start coming into play.

SHC at $15.66 is the smallest of the three. Target $16.96, stop $14.79. It's a lottery ticket by our desk's standards, not a core position. Size it accordingly.

The common thread across all three: they're not momentum chases. They're pullback buys in names that already flushed and are now turning. That fits the week's thesis perfectly. The flush happened. The turn is underway. These are the names where the turn is showing up in the signals, not in someone's gut feeling.

One thing to keep honest about. VIX at 18.97 is comfortable but not complacent. Sub-15 would be complacent. Sub-19 after a flush is the market catching its breath. It can stay here and drift higher for weeks. The danger zone for this thesis isn't a spike back to 25. It's a slow grind where VIX sits at 17-18 and everyone forgets they were scared five days ago. That's when the next flush sets up. We're not there yet.

What I'm watching into Friday. Two things. First, SPY 735. That's the line where this week's flush reversal either holds or fails. A close below 735 on Thursday or Friday means the bounce was a dead cat, not a real turn. Second, Friday's jobs data. If it comes in soft, the flush thesis gets a second tailwind from rate-cut expectations. If it comes in hot, the VIX pop will test every stop on the board. Size for the second scenario. Hope for the first.

The crowd saw a top on Saturday. The tape said no. Now we find out if the tape meant it.
