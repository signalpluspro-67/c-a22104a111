The Jobs Report Changed the Math

172,000 reasons the Fed will not cut, and how I am positioned for it
Week of 2026-06-05
The ThesisA hot labor print pulled rate cuts off the table, yields jumped, and the crowded mega-cap trade took its first real hit. I stay heavy cash and lean into the rate-insensitive corners that just outperformed.

Last week I told you the top had been postponed. This Friday the postponement ended. The May jobs report came in at 172,000 against 85,000 expected, more than double, and a labor market that strong is good news for the economy and bad news for a market priced on rate cuts. Yields ripped, the ten-year cleared 4.5%, and hike odds for this year jumped past half. The index gave back 2.6%, the Nasdaq 4.2%.

📊 Market Health

The damage was surgical, not broad. The index fell hard but the median stock barely moved and more than half held above the 50-day. What came off was the froth at the very top.

🧠 My Take

I still hold the heaviest cash of my year and this week I am glad of it. I am not selling the hole, the field is holding. But I am not chasing either. I put cash to work slowly, only in names that proved Friday they do not need the Fed. Cautious ~55%, constructive ~45%.
