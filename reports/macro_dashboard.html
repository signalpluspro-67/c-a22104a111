The macro regime sits at 10/100, a score that masks a sharp internal contradiction. On the surface, financial conditions remain accommodative: the St. Louis FSI at -0.76, the Chicago Fed FCI at -0.52, and HY spreads essentially at historic lows all suggest markets are not pricing stress. Yet the real economy is absorbing a significant energy shock. WTI at $112.25 represents a 72.6% year-over-year surge and a 30.7% single-month move, feeding directly into a PPI reading of 9.82% and re-accelerating CPI at 3.95%. The Fed has not moved. The macro regime is low not because things are breaking, but because the data sends contradictory signals simultaneously. That tension will resolve either through Fed action or demand destruction.
