Options · Pricing
Volatility Skew
The pattern of implied volatility across strikes — equity puts are systematically richer than calls.
  • Steep skew = market pricing tail-risk on the downside
  • Flat or inverted skew = complacency or speculative call buying
  • Track 25-delta risk reversal to quantify
Cited when discussing VIX SKEW index, hedging cost regimes, tail-risk pricing.
Options · Positioning
GEX & Dealer Gamma
Gamma Exposure measures how dealer hedging amplifies or dampens price moves around the spot.
  • +GEX: dealers buy dips, sell rips → mean-reverting
  • −GEX: dealers chase price → momentum, gap risk
  • Zero-gamma flip = spot price where regime switches
Cited when reading SPX dealer positioning, OPEX pin-risk weeks, vol regime calls.
Options · Edge
IV vs HV Divergence
The gap between implied (market expectation) and realized (what happened) volatility.
  • IV > HV: options overpriced → short-vol favored
  • IV < HV: options underpriced → long-vol favored
  • VRP = long-run structural premium (~3-4%)
Used in the VRP screener and earnings-IV setups.
Options · Levels
Put Walls & Call Walls
Strikes with abnormally large open interest that act as gravity points for price.
  • Put wall below spot = support floor
  • Call wall above spot = resistance ceiling
  • Walls shift weekly with new OI — check before OPEX
Cited for weekly key levels, OPEX pin analysis, tactical bounds.
Stats · Multiple Testing
Bonferroni Correction
When testing many hypotheses, false-positive risk explodes — Bonferroni scales the threshold to compensate.
  • Test N hypotheses → threshold = α / N (very conservative)
  • Eliminates ~95% of backtest p-hacking artifacts
  • Use FDR (Benjamini-Hochberg) when N is very large
Filters the 22 Kerberos signals and the 1023-combo confluence lab.
Stats · Significance
Permutation Test (MCPT)
Shuffle signal labels thousands of times — how often does noise produce the observed Sharpe?
  • Destroy signal-label link N times (5000+ shuffles)
  • p-value = fraction of permutations beating observed
  • Robust to non-normality (essential in finance)
Step 2 of the 8-step doctrine — every signal claim requires this.
Stats · Time-Series
Block Bootstrap
Resample contiguous blocks of returns instead of individual days — preserves the autocorrelation that destroys naive bootstraps.
  • Block size ≈ typical autocorr horizon (5-20 days)
  • 100% of Bonferroni-passing signals can still fail here
  • Cluster-busting: one lucky regime ≠ systematic edge
The kill-switch step after Bonferroni — most signals die here.
Stats · Labels
Triple Barrier (López de Prado)
Label each trade by which barrier hits first: profit-take, stop-loss, or time-out — three exit paths, three outcomes.
  • +1 (win): TP hits first — keep size
  • −1 (loss): SL hits first — exit, learn
  • 0 (timeout): ambiguous — flat or partial
Used in PI for meta-labeling and realistic exit modeling vs return-only.
Stats · Cross-Validation
Purged K-Fold
Standard K-fold leaks information through overlapping labels — purging removes that leakage so OOS scores are honest.
  • Purge: drop training samples whose labels overlap test
  • Embargo: skip a buffer after test to prevent serial leakage
  • PULLBACK Sharpe stays +1.82 after purging — survivor
PI used this to find PULLBACK survives, BOTTOM/BREAKOUT break (May 2026).
Stats · Overfitting
DSR & PBO (López de Prado)
Deflated Sharpe Ratio + Probability of Backtest Overfitting — the two metrics that price-in your selection bias.
  • DSR shrinks Sharpe by # trials and return skew/kurt
  • PBO > 50% = backtest likely random luck
  • Both required for AFML-grade signal acceptance
Step 6 of the 8-step doctrine. See Robustness Lab in screeners_overview.

Cup & Handle
A U-shaped consolidation (cup) followed by a short, shallow pullback (handle) before the breakout.

  • Buy: pivot break with volume ≥ 40% above average

Options · Greeks
The Five Greeks
Δ Γ Θ V ρ — five letters that govern every options P&L attribution.
  • Δ: directional exposure (price)
  • Γ: convexity (rate of Δ change)
  • Θ: time decay · V: vol sensitivity · ρ: rate sensitivity
Read in conjunction with position sizing and IV regime.
Options · Hedging
Delta Hedging
Continuously offset your delta exposure by trading the underlying — what dealers do, every day.
  • Long gamma → buy low, sell high (mean-revert)
  • Short gamma → chase price (amplify)
  • Hedging cost = realized vol × |gamma| × dt
Why GEX regime shapes daily price action.
Options · Decay
Theta Decay Curve
Option time-value erodes non-linearly — almost flat far from expiry, brutal in the last 30 days.
  • Theta accelerates inside ~21 DTE (gamma's mirror)
  • Sellers prefer 30-45 DTE entries; close at 21
  • Long options: avoid holding into last 2 weeks unless directional
PI uses 60-75 DTE for lottery plays (theta tolerable).
Options · Convexity
Gamma — Convexity of Δ
Gamma measures how fast delta changes — peaks at ATM, falls off OTM and ITM.
  • ATM gamma is max — small moves swing delta hard
  • Short-dated options have highest gamma (riskier to short)
  • Gamma squeeze = forced delta hedging cascade
Differs from GEX (dealer-level positioning aggregate).
Options · Vol Sensitivity
Vega — IV Sensitivity
How much an option price moves for a 1-point change in implied volatility.
  • Long vega = wants IV to rise (long straddle, long calls)
  • Short vega = wants IV to fall (credit spreads, iron condors)
  • Vega > theta for >90 DTE positions (vol regime matters)
IV regime → which side of vega to be on. See IV vs HV.
Options · Event
IV Crush
After earnings or any binary event, implied vol collapses overnight — long options bleed even if direction is right.
  • IV inflates pre-event 2-5×, collapses to baseline post-event
  • Earnings: avg crush ~30-50% in 1 day
  • Sellers earn the premium; buyers need a huge move
Why PI flags "avoid earnings IV chase" in screener alerts.

Stats · Risk-adjusted
Sharpe vs Sortino vs Calmar
Three risk-adjusted return ratios — each penalizes a different thing.
  • Sharpe penalizes upside vol (unfair)
  • Sortino: only downside vol (more honest)
  • Calmar: return / max drawdown (capital preservation)
PI ranks systems on all three + DSR.
Stats · Memory
Hurst Exponent
A number between 0 and 1 that says: is this series mean-reverting, random, or trending?
  • H ~ 0.5 → markets are noise (mostly true)
  • H < 0.4 → fade extremes (mean-reversion zone)
  • H > 0.6 → ride trends (trend-following zone)
In PI's Sinclair Edge Modules for regime classification.
Stats · Time-Series
ACF — Autocorrelation
How correlated is today's return with yesterday's? With last week's? Plot the answer.
  • ACF outside the bands at lag k → memory at horizon k
  • Daily returns: ACF ≈ 0 (random); abs returns: ACF > 0 (vol clustering)
  • Foundation for block bootstrap block size
Used to validate stationarity before backtest.
Stats · Filter
CUSUM Filter
A change-point detector that fires when cumulative deviation crosses a threshold — generates structural events.
  • Detects regime shifts without arbitrary calendar buckets
  • López de Prado: use CUSUM events instead of every bar
  • Reduces overfitting from sampling noise
Building block for event-driven backtests.
Stats · ML
Meta-Labeling
Train a second model to decide whether to TAKE the trade your first model triggered. The "should I act?" filter.
  • Primary: directional (long/short/flat)
  • Meta: confidence (skip if low)
  • Boosts precision; AFML Ch.3 staple
PI applied this to PULLBACK — meta-model REJECT after rigorous validation.
Stats · Memory
Fractional Differentiation
Take a fractional derivative of a price series — keeps stationarity AND memory, unlike simple differencing.
  • d=1 (returns) kills all memory
  • d=0 (prices) is non-stationary (no ML possible)
  • 0 < d < 1 = sweet spot for features
López de Prado AFML Ch.5 — for ML on time-series.

New Highs − New Lows
Net new 52-week highs each day. Persistent positivity = expansion; flips negative = contraction.
