Introducing HPK

HighPeak Energy is an independent crude oil and natural gas exploration and production company. It operates exclusively in the Permian Basin across West Texas and Eastern New Mexico. The company went public in 2020 and is headquartered in Fort Worth, Texas.

The Permian is the most economic oil basin in the United States. HighPeak focuses on the Howard County area, where well economics compete with the best in the basin. The company runs a lean operation with roughly 50 employees, which tells you this is a tight, founder-led shop.

HighPeak's edge is basin selection and operating efficiency. The challenge is scale and balance sheet. The upside comes when crude cooperates. The downside comes fast when it does not.

Risk Factors

Crude softness is the primary risk. WTI already rolled over, dropping roughly 2.2% on July 9, which dragged HPK down 7.1% over 21 days. A further slide in oil pushes the whole group lower. HighPeak is a levered oil-price proxy. When crude falls, the debt load amplifies the equity pain.

Dilution is the second risk. The company has a live $150M ATM equity offering. That means shares hit the market at prevailing prices, capping upside. PE-partnership share distributions to LPs add more supply. Both overhangs can absorb buying pressure and keep the stock from reaching the 8.30 target.

Liquidity is the third risk. The stock trades only $3.8M in daily dollar volume. ATR is 0.42. Slippage on entry and exit will eat your edge if you size up. Keep position size small or you become the market.

The invalidation is a daily close below 5.82. That breaks the pullback thesis and the rising 30-week support. The swing low is 5.59, so 5.82 is the last defense before a test of that floor.

The timing risk around earnings is real. The print lands in 25 days. If you enter now at 7.29, you carry into a binary event where the last quarter showed a $25.2M loss and a suspended dividend. The market may price in disappointment before the print. If you wait for the 6.66 entry, you may not get filled before earnings. That is the bind.
