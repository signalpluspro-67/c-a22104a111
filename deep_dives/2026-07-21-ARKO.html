Introducing ARKO

Arko Corp. runs one of the largest independent convenience store chains in the United States. The company operates through four segments: Retail, Wholesale, Fleet Fueling, and GPMP. Retail is the visible part. Those are the stores selling fuel, cigarettes, beverages, prepared foods, and grocery items to everyday consumers. Wholesale supplies fuel to dealers and sub-wholesalers. Fleet Fueling runs cardlock locations and sells fuel through proprietary cards. GPMP handles wholesale fuel distribution internally.

That makes it a small cap. The business model is high-volume, low-margin fuel sales anchored by higher-margin merchandise inside the store. Scale matters here. Arko has enough locations to negotiate fuel costs and enough foot traffic to make the merchandise math work.

The edge is acquisition-driven growth. Arko has rolled up independent convenience store operators for years, expanding its footprint without building from scratch. That strategy adds revenue quickly but absorbs debt and integration risk. You are buying a roll-up story trading at a low sales multiple with real free cash flow, not a pristine balance sheet.
