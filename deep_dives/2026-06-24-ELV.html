The Big Picture

Managed care is the cheapest corner of an expensive market, and that gap is the whole reason to look here. Elevance trades at a forward price-to-earnings of 13.3 and a price-to-sales of 0.4. You pay forty cents for a dollar of revenue from a company that books over $200 billion in sales a year. That is value math you rarely find in a broad market priced near records.

Health benefits is a slow, regulated, low-margin business. Net margins sit at 2.6%. The model wins on scale, not on flash. You insure tens of millions of lives, you collect premiums, you manage the medical loss ratio, and you compound book value over decades. Book value per share is 201.97, and the stock trades at 1.9 times that. The market is not paying for a dream. It is paying for a utility-like cash machine with a 0.68 beta, meaning the stock moves about two-thirds as hard as the broad market on any given day.

The sector itself is flat. Healthcare reads NEUTRAL right now, no acceleration, no tailwind. That cuts both ways. It means Elevance is not riding a hot group, so the move it just made came on its own legs. A name that runs 36.1% over 63 trading days while its sector goes nowhere is doing something the rest of the group is not.

That is the backdrop that rewards a name like this. Cheap multiple, defensive cash flow, low beta, and relative strength inside a group that offers no help. You want the stock that works when its neighbors do not.

Introducing $ELV

Elevance Health is the company that used to be called Anthem. It changed its name in June 2022, and it runs one of the largest Blue Cross Blue Shield franchises in the country. You know it through the Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield, Wellpoint, and Carelon brands.

The business splits into four pieces. Health Benefits is the core insurance arm, covering individual, employer, Medicare, Medicaid, and federal members. CarelonRx is the pharmacy services engine, handling home delivery, specialty pharmacy, formulary management, and rebates. Carelon Services wraps the clinical side, behavioral health, care management, palliative care, virtual care, and the HealthOS data platform. Corporate and Other holds the rest.

The edge is vertical. Elevance does not only sell the insurance policy. It increasingly owns the pharmacy benefit and the care management around it, which means it captures margin that used to flow to outside vendors. The CarelonRx and Carelon Services build-out is the company telling you it wants to be the pharmacy benefit manager and the care coordinator, not just the payer writing checks. That is where the future operating leverage lives.
