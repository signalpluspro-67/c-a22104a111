Introducing SXC

SunCoke Energy is the largest independent producer of coke in the United States. It operates through two segments: Domestic Coke and Industrial Services. The Domestic Coke segment makes blast-furnace and foundry coke at company-owned cokemaking facilities. The Industrial Services segment handles material handling, scrap processing, slag processing, logistics, and environmental services for steel producers, utilities, and bulk-material customers.

The edge here is asset specificity. SunCoke owns and operates cokemaking facilities and logistics terminals that are expensive to replicate and tied to long-term customer relationships. Its industrial-services business is embedded inside customer operations, running scrap yards, melt shops, and material-handling logistics on-site at steel mills. That embedded position creates switching costs and recurring revenue. The company also markets co-products from cokemaking, which adds a secondary revenue stream from the same production process.

You're buying a capital-intensive industrial company with real leverage, contracted revenue, and a services moat wrapped around its core cokemaking assets.
