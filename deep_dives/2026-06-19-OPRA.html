Introducing $OPRA

Opera Limited builds web browsers and the services that ride on top of them. It runs Opera One, the gaming-focused Opera GX, and Opera Neon, a subscription agentic AI browser aimed at people who want their browser to do work for them. The company was founded in 1995 and is headquartered in Oslo, Norway, with 605 employees. It is a subsidiary of Kunlun Tech.

The money comes from more than the browser itself. Opera Ads is an online advertising platform, and Opera News is an AI-powered news discovery service that monetizes attention. The company also has Web3 and e-commerce pieces, plus GameMaker, a 2D game development tool. The browser is the distribution engine, and the ads and search revenue are the payoff.

The edge here is focus and reach without the cost base of a giant. Opera does not need to win the browser war outright. It needs to keep a loyal, monetizable user base, especially in gaming and emerging-market segments, and convert that engagement into ad and search dollars. With a market cap near 1.59B and gross margins at 51.0%, it runs lean and keeps a large share of every dollar of revenue.
