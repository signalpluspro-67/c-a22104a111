Introducing AEHR

Aehr Test Systems makes burn-in and test equipment for semiconductor devices. The company's core products are the FOX-XP and FOX-NP systems, which test and stabilize devices at the wafer level and as singulated die. That matters because testing before packaging catches defects earlier and saves money.

Aehr's edge is silicon carbide. SiC power semiconductors require extensive burn-in to prove reliability, and Aehr's WaferPak and DiePak systems handle that workload for customers pushing into EV power electronics and advanced sensors. The company is headquartered in Fremont, California, and was incorporated in 1977.

That combination of thin float and high beta (3.18) means the stock moves hard on catalysts. The gap you are looking at is real, and so is the risk of a fade.

The Thesis

The core thesis is post-earnings drift. Aehr reported fiscal Q4 and full-year 2026 earnings on July 14, 2026, gapping from roughly $72 to $110 on a beat and a blowout fiscal 2027 guide of $130 to $150M in revenue versus roughly $85M consensus. Post-earnings-announcement drift, or PEAD, is the tendency for stocks to keep moving in the direction of a surprise after an initial gap. The desk's historical study of this pattern produced a validated risk-adjusted return of 1.10, and that edge has been tested in live conditions.

Three independent desk reads agree on the bullish setup. The stock sits 15.2% below its 52-week high of $126.62, which gives it room to run. Call flow printed 3360 contracts on July 20, 2026.
