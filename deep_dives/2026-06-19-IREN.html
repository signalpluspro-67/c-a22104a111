Crypto is one of the hottest themes on the desk this session, and that matters for how a name like this trades. When the theme runs, the operators with real infrastructure get rewarded first, then the speculative shells follow. IREN sits in the first group.

Introducing $IREN

IREN Limited runs a vertically integrated data center business across Canada and Australia. It owns its computing hardware, its electrical infrastructure, and the physical sites. On top of that it mines Bitcoin, the scarce digital asset created through a global peer-to-peer network.

The company was incorporated in 2018 and is based in Sydney, Australia. It carried the name Iris Energy until November 2024, when it rebranded to IREN. The headcount is lean at 257 employees, which tells you this is a capital and power business, not a labor-heavy one.

The edge is ownership. Most crypto-adjacent names rent their capacity or chase the cheapest hosting deal of the month. IREN owns the stack from the substation to the rack. That control is what lets a Bitcoin miner pivot toward high-value compute without starting from zero. The same power contracts and the same buildings serve both jobs.
