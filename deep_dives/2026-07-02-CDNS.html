Introducing $CDNS

Cadence Design Systems builds the software that engineers use to design and verify chips before manufacturing. The core franchise is electronic design automation. Tools like Jasper for formal verification, Xcelium for logic simulation, Palladium for emulation, and the Virtuoso platform for custom analog design run inside nearly every serious chip company on the planet. The switching costs are enormous because entire engineering teams train on these tools for years.

The second leg is design intellectual property. Cadence licenses ready-made building blocks: PCI Express controllers, USB, memory interfaces, SerDes, and the Tensilica DSP cores. Chip designers drop these blocks into their layouts instead of building from scratch. That business scales with the number of chips being designed, which keeps climbing.

The newer push is system design and analysis. Cadence now sells tools for the physics around the chip: thermal analysis with Celsius, signal integrity with Sigrity X, computational fluid dynamics with Fidelity, and electromagnetic simulation with the Clarity 3D solver. It is a business that sells picks and shovels to everyone building the future of compute, and it collects on both the tools and the blocks.
