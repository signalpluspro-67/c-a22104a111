Introducing ELV

Elevance Health, formerly Anthem, is one of the largest health benefits companies in the United States. It operates through four segments: Health Benefits, CarelonRx, Carelon Services, and Corporate & Other. The company serves individual, employer group, Medicare, Medicaid, and Federal Employee Program members under the Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield, Wellpoint, and Carelon brands.

The business model is straightforward. ELV collects premiums, manages medical costs, and keeps the spread. It also runs a pharmacy benefit operation through CarelonRx and offers specialty services like behavioral health, palliative care, and payment integrity. The scale is enormous.

The edge here is size and diversification. A company this large, with a Medicaid franchise, a PBM, and a services platform, has multiple revenue engines. That diversification normally smooths out earnings volatility. It also means a problem in one segment can drag the whole machine down. That is exactly what happened in Q2.
