The Big Picture

Healthcare is not leading this market. The sector reads NEUTRAL and its momentum is DECELERATING, which means broad sector beta will not carry a position for you. That pushes you toward stocks that move on their own clinical path instead of riding the group.

Radiation oncology is where the interesting small-cap work sits. Most external-beam radiation uses photons or, in advanced centers, protons. Both work, both have limits near sensitive tissue. Alpha-particle therapy aims at a different physics problem: deliver a short-range, high-energy dose directly inside a tumor, then let it diffuse only millimeters before it stops. The promise is a harder hit on the cancer with less collateral damage to what surrounds it.

That physics matters most for solid tumors in awkward places. Pancreas, prostate, head and neck, skin. These are exactly the indications where a localized alpha dose could change outcomes, and exactly where a small, focused company can build an edge before larger players notice.

A backdrop like this rewards a company with its own trial-driven story and a chart already trending, because you are not relying on the sector to do the lifting.

Introducing $DRTS

Alpha Tau Medical ($DRTS) is a clinical-stage oncology company based in Jerusalem, Israel, with roughly 121 employees. Its product is Alpha DaRT, short for diffusing alpha-emitters radiation therapy. The idea is to seed a tumor with tiny sources that release alpha particles, killing cancer from the inside over a controlled radius.

The pipeline is broad for a company this size. Alpha DaRT is in clinical trials across skin, oral, pancreatic, prostate, lung, liver, and breast cancer, with preclinical work behind it. The company also holds a strategic collaboration to develop and commercialize the technology for prostate cancer, which gives it a partner and a focused commercial target.

This is a single-technology platform company. The bet is that one physical approach to radiation can be aimed at many cancers. That is the upside and the concentration risk in one sentence.
