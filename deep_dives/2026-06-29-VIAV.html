The Big Picture

The optical layer is where the AI buildout actually lives. Every datacenter that trains a model moves data between chips, racks, and buildings over light. That light has to be generated, coated, measured, and verified, and the companies that supply the test gear and the optical components sit in front of a demand wave that is still building.

Photonics is one of tonight's hot themes for a reason. Optical interconnects are scaling inside AI clusters. Co-packaged optics, higher-speed transceivers, and denser fiber routes all need validation before they ship and monitoring once they run. Spend on network test and assurance tends to track that complexity, not the price of any single chip.

There is a second leg most people miss. Optical coatings power 3D sensing in phones, anti-counterfeiting in currency, and filters in aerospace and defense. That hardware is unglamorous and high-margin, and it throws off cash through cycles that have nothing to do with the AI trade.

This backdrop rewards a name that sells the picks and shovels of the light layer instead of betting on one end market. Viavi does exactly that.

Introducing $VIAV

Viavi Solutions is the company you used to know as JDS Uniphase. The rebrand split the old telecom-bubble story into a focused test-and-optics business.

It runs two segments. Network and Service Enablement (NSE) builds the instruments, software, and assurance tools that telecom carriers, cloud operators, militaries, and first responders use to develop, deploy, and police their networks. This is the test-equipment franchise: lab gear, field probes, service assurance, and the AIOps software that watches live networks for trouble.

The second segment, Optical Security and Performance Products (OSP), is the quiet cash engine. It takes Viavi's core optical-coating technology and turns it into anti-counterfeiting features for banknotes, 3D-sensing filters for consumer devices, and components for government, aerospace, automotive, and industrial customers. The company has also signed a partnership to develop quantum security and network intelligence, a long-dated option on where this all goes.

The edge here is hard-won manufacturing. Optical coatings at volume are difficult to copy, and decades of carrier relationships make Viavi a default vendor when a network operator needs to certify new hardware.

The Thesis

The honest read first: the story is strong and the entry is gone. Two independent signals lined up on a photonics name sitting 12.9% below its 52-week high with a neutral momentum reading, and that combination is what surfaced Viavi to the desk. The chart is in a clean uptrend, the theme has a tailwind, and the options are tradeable. On paper this is a high-quality long.

The problem is price. The setup was designed to enter near 41.77, where the math worked: about 32.5% of upside to the 55.33 target against 20.4% of risk to the 33.23 stop. Viavi now trades at 48.18. That is roughly 15% above the intended entry and inside spitting distance of the target.

So size this for what it is. From 48.18 you have only about 15% left to 55.33 while the stop sits about 31% below you. The designed reward-to-risk of 1.6 to 1 has inverted at the current chase price.

Relative strength against the broad market is strong at about +22.6% over the same 63 days, so the digestion is happening from a position of leadership.

Use the desk's levels as your map. Support and the real reference for buyers is the 41.77 area, with the 35.87 swing low and the 33.23 stop beneath it. Overhead, 55.33 is the swing-high target and the line that matters; a daily close through it on expanding volume would open the path toward 60.98, just under the 60.43 52-week high.

With ATR around 4.27, a normal day swings more than four dollars. That matters for stop placement. A 33.23 stop is more than three average ranges below the current price, which is why chasing here ties up a wide, expensive stop for a thin slice of remaining upside.

Why Now

The near-term clock is earnings. That report is the catalyst that either confirms the 42.8% revenue trajectory and the path to forward profitability or breaks it. There is no other verified event in the window, so the timing question is really a question about positioning into that print.

The options tape leans bullish but lightly. On 2026-06-26, calls traded 610 contracts against 193 puts, and call premium of about 0.29 million dollars outran put premium of roughly 0.09 million. That is a clean three-to-one skew toward upside, the kind of flow that fits a stock holding a tight consolidation near its highs while traders position for the next leg.

The macro frame is supportive on the index level and mixed underneath. The broad market is in a constructive regime, and photonics is a live theme. The catch sits in the sector internals: Technology reads neutral, and the sector's near-term momentum has rolled to a weakening posture. So the tailwind is the theme and the broad tape, while the sector group itself is no longer accelerating. That argues for selectivity and against chasing the whole tech complex into this print.

Risk Factors

Technology is the stretched part of this market, and Viavi carries a beta of about 1.19. A broad tech pullback drags it down with the group regardless of how good the photonics story is, and the sector's weakening near-term momentum makes that the most credible way you lose money here.

The second risk is the one you create by chasing. At 48.18 the trade is mechanically stale. Price already ran past the 41.77 entry, the reward-to-risk that justified the setup has inverted, and a buy here pairs about 15% of remaining upside with a stop more than 30% below. Pay the chase and you are accepting a wide, expensive stop for a thin reward.

The valuation amplifies any disappointment. Forward P/E near 41.8 and price-to-sales of 9.7 price in a successful turn to the forward EPS of 1.28. The leverage, with debt-to-equity at 1.34, leaves less room for error than the cash flow alone suggests.

The single invalidation is the 33.23 stop. A close below it ends the thesis and confirms the stage-two uptrend has broken.

- The catch: at 48.18 the 1.6-to-1 setup has inverted, so this is a wait-for-the-dip name, not a chase.

Conclusion

Viavi is a genuine photonics turnaround with the revenue, the cash flow, and the chart to back the story.

My stance is straightforward and a little contrarian to the headline. You like the company and you wait for the price. The 41.77 entry was the trade; at 48.18 you are paying up for 15% of remaining upside against a stop 31% below, and that math does not earn full size. Patience is the edge here, because the business is not going anywhere and the next pullback gives you a cleaner shot at the same upside.
