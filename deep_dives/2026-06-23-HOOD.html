The Big Picture

The retail brokerage business changed shape over the past two years, and Robinhood is the cleanest expression of that shift. The model moved past zero-commission trading and into a wider product set: margin, securities lending, cash sweep, retirement accounts, crypto, event contracts, and around-the-clock trading. Each of those lines turns a trading app into something closer to a full financial account, and each one carries higher margin than the order flow business that built the company.

Financial Services as a group has run hard. The sector sits in an EXHAUSTED state, which means the easy part of the move is behind it and the tailwind that carried capital markets names is fading. That matters for how you size this. You are buying into a group that already paid out, so the bet here rests on the individual name, the insider, and the discount, rather than on a rising tide lifting everything.

Two themes still pull money toward Robinhood specifically: FinTech and Crypto. Both are cyclical, both swing with risk appetite, and both reward operators that own the customer relationship instead of renting it. Robinhood owns the account, the app, and increasingly the wallet and the credit card. When sentiment turns up, this is the kind of name that captures the inflow first.

That backdrop rewards a business with high incremental margins, real cash generation, and a customer base that deepens its product use over time. Robinhood checks those boxes. The question is price, and right now price gives you a name that already corrected hard while the operator inside the building was buying.

Introducing $HOOD

Robinhood Markets runs a financial services platform in the United States. Customers invest in stocks, ETFs, and American depositary receipts, with fractional trading, recurring investments, margin access, and fully-paid securities lending layered on top. The platform now stretches into retirement accounts, joint investing, cash sweep, instant withdrawals, event contracts, futures, and short selling.

The edge is the account itself. Once a customer holds cash, securities, crypto, a Robinhood credit card, and a spending account in one place, the switching cost rises and the revenue per user climbs. The education layer (Snacks, Learn, In-App Education, Crypto Learn and Earn) keeps newer investors inside the app and lowers the cost of activating the next product. That flywheel is why a brokerage trades at the multiples Robinhood carries.

The financial profile reads more like software than like a traditional broker. Gross margin runs at 92.2%, operating margin at 38.5%, and net profit margin at 41.1%. Those numbers explain both the appeal and the risk: the business prints cash when activity is high, and it gives that operating leverage back fast when activity cools.
