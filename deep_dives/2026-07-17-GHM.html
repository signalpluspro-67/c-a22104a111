The Big Picture

Introducing GHM

Graham Corporation designs and manufactures vacuum and heat-transfer equipment. The company was founded in 1936 and is headquartered in Batavia, New York. It employs 732 people. Its machinery serves chemical and petrochemical processing, petroleum refining, defense, energy, and space.

The defense side is where Graham builds torpedo ejection systems, propulsion components, turbines, and alternators. The space side is where the growth narrative lives. Graham supplies turbopumps, cryogenic pumps, cooling systems, and life-support hardware for rocket propulsion. These are mission-critical components with high switching costs and deep qualification requirements.

Graham also produces advanced mixing systems and thermal management hardware for defense and energy customers. The company serves clients across the United States, Asia, Canada, the Middle East, and South America. It is a small-cap industrial with a specialized engineering edge, and that edge is what attracted the desk's attention.
