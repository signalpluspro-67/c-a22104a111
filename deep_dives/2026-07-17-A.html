Introducing ${A}

Agilent Technologies is a $37.8 billion life-sciences instruments company headquartered in Santa Clara, California. It was spun out of Hewlett-Packard in 1999 and has built a focused franchise in analytical instruments, diagnostics, and lab services.

The business runs through three segments. Life Sciences and Diagnostics Markets sells liquid chromatography, mass spectrometry, genomics, and companion diagnostics. Agilent CrossLab provides repairs, maintenance, consumables, and software. Applied Markets covers gas chromatography, spectroscopy, and vacuum technology. The CrossLab segment is the sticky one. Once a lab installs Agilent instruments, the consumables and service contracts create a recurring revenue stream that compounds over time.

That installed-base economics is the edge. Labs do not switch instruments lightly. Validation, training, and data continuity all lock customers in. Agilent turns that lock-in into 52.6% gross margins and 23.7% operating margins. The company is a quiet compounder in a sector that rewards scale and reputation.
