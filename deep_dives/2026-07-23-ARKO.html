Introducing ARKO

Arko Corp. runs one of the largest independent convenience store chains in the United States. The company operates through four segments. Retail sells fuel, merchandise, prepared foods, beverages, and tobacco directly to consumers. Wholesale supplies fuel to dealers and sub-wholesalers. Fleet Fueling runs cardlock locations and sells fuel through proprietary cards. GPMP handles wholesale fuel distribution across the retail and wholesale segments.
Arko moves a massive volume of fuel and captures margin at multiple points in the chain. The wholesale and fleet segments give it revenue stability that pure retail c-store operators lack.
You are buying a fuel-distribution business with a retail overlay. The retail side drives brand visibility and merchandise margin. The wholesale side drives volume. Together they produce $6.49 billion in trailing revenue from a company with a market cap under $1 billion.

Risk Factors

Forward EPS of $0.05 versus trailing EPS of $0.20 signals analyst expectation of significant earnings decline. If that decline materializes, the forward P/E of 158.8 becomes the anchor and the stock reprices lower.
