Introducing BSX

Boston Scientific develops, manufactures, and markets medical devices across two segments: MedSurg and Cardiovascular. The MedSurg segment covers gastrointestinal, urological, and neurological products. The Cardiovascular segment handles the diagnosis and treatment of heart diseases, including its WATCHMAN FLX left atrial appendage closure device and various implantable cardiac monitors and defibrillators.

The company's edge has historically been its footprint in high-growth niches like electrophysiology and structural heart repair. The WATCHMAN device became a flagship product, driving significant revenue growth as it captured share in stroke prevention for atrial fibrillation patients. Its broad portfolio across multiple specialties provides diversification within a single med-tech name.

The current distress in the stock stems from concerns about exactly that growth engine. The desk's bear case notes management cut 2026 organic growth guidance to approximately 6.5% to 8%, citing slowing Watchman adoption, competition in electrophysiology, and soft hospital capital spending. A July pump recall added to the pressure. Analysts at Evercore, Truist, RBC, and BTIG slashed their targets between July 6 and July 14. You are looking at a stock the Street has abandoned.
