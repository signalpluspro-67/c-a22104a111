Introducing CMP

Compass Minerals International mines and sells two things: salt and plant nutrition. The Salt segment produces rock salt, evaporated salt, and magnesium chloride for highway deicing, water treatment, chemical production, and consumer use. This is the core business. Highways need salt every winter. The revenue is recurring and weather-driven.

The Plant Nutrition segment makes sulfate of potash specialty fertilizers under the Protassium+ brand. These products go to distributors, retailers, and growers who need low-chloride fertilizer for high-value crops. The segment is smaller but carries higher margins and growth potential.

The company was founded in 1844 and is headquartered in Overland Park, Kansas. The salt business gives you steady cash flow. The plant nutrition business gives you upside. You are buying a $1.25B market-cap company with real assets, real revenue, and a narrow moat in essential minerals. The edge is in the assets. You cannot manufacture a salt mine quickly or cheaply. The barriers to entry are physical.
