The Big Picture

Utilities are leading this market, and that is a strange place to find leadership. The group sits in a momentum state and at the early stage of its acceleration. Early strength like this matters because you are stepping into a trend with room to extend rather than one already exhausted.

The driver is electricity. Data centers, AI training, and crypto mining all consume enormous power, and the companies that own generation and grid capacity are the ones collecting the checks. Independent power producers sit at the front of that trade. They can sign contracts with industrial buyers who pay for certainty, and they sell into energy markets that have grown tighter as demand keeps climbing.

This is why a sleepy, defensive sector suddenly trades like a growth theme. The utility label hides a real shift. Power is the bottleneck for everything the digital economy wants to do next.

That backdrop rewards a name that owns the power and rents it to the buyers who need it most. Digi Power X is exactly that kind of hybrid, and it comes to you 21.3% below its high while its sector pushes forward.

Introducing $DGXX

Digi Power X builds data centers and the energy assets that feed them. It runs three lines of business: cryptocurrency mining, sales of energy and electricity, and colocation services where it rents out space and power to industrial clients.

The company was Digihost Technology until March 2025, when it renamed to Digi Power X to mark the move from a pure crypto miner toward an energy infrastructure operator.

The edge is the combination. Owning both the power and the racks gives the company a switch. It can mine when crypto economics are rich and lean on colocation revenue when they are not. That flexibility is the entire pitch, and it is why the market is willing to treat a small operator as a piece of the data-center buildout rather than a commodity miner.

You are buying a momentum continuation, paid for by a balance sheet, not by earnings.

Risk Factors

The invalidation is clean. A daily close below 5.22 voids the trade. So does a break of the uptrend structure or any real adverse catalyst that surfaces, since none has been ruled out.
