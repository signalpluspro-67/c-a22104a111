Introducing GS

Goldman Sachs is a global financial institution operating across three segments: Global Banking & Markets, Asset & Wealth Management, and Platform Solutions. The Global Banking & Markets segment drives the majority of its revenue through advisory work on mergers and acquisitions, equity and debt underwriting, and institutional client execution across credit, rates, currencies, commodities, and equities. Asset & Wealth Management manages capital across equity, fixed income, hedge funds, private equity, real estate, and infrastructure. Platform Solutions handles transaction banking, deposit-taking, and payment solutions for corporate and institutional clients.

The company was founded in 1869 and is headquartered in New York. Its edge sits in the franchise. When dealmaking booms, Goldman captures advisory and underwriting fees at the top of the league tables. When markets get volatile, its trading desks benefit from wider spreads and higher client flow. You are buying a leveraged play on capital markets activity, priced at a premium because the franchise commands it.

The business model is inherently cyclical. Revenue depends on deal flow, trading volumes, and asset levels. That is normal for a bank holding company, but it means you are trusting risk management as much as earnings power.
