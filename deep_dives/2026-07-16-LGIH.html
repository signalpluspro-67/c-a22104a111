The Big Picture

The residential construction trade lives and dies on mortgage rates. When the 10-year yield drops, homebuilders rip. When it spikes, they get crushed. That sensitivity is the entire investment case in one sentence.

LGI Homes sits in the affordable end of the market. Entry-level buyers need financing help. Builders like LGI absorb some of that cost through rate buydowns. It works when rates are stable or falling. It hurts when rates jump and cancellations rise.

The macro read here is neutral. The Consumer Cyclical sector is marked NEUTRAL with a DECELERATING acceleration signal. That means the tailwind that drove homebuilders earlier is fading. You are buying a stock that already ran 55.4% in three months into a sector whose momentum is cooling.

Introducing LGIH

LGI Homes builds entry-level homes in 20 states. The company targets first-time buyers and active adults with its LGI Homes brand, and luxury buyers under the Terrata Homes brand. It also sells homes in bulk to institutions looking for single-family rental properties.

The business model is volume-driven. LGI builds affordable homes at scale, sells them quickly, and keeps inventory turning. The company operates in Texas, Arizona, Florida, Georgia, and other high-growth Sun Belt markets. Headquartered in The Woodlands, Texas, it was founded in 2003 and employs roughly 1,056 people.

The edge is cost control and a specific customer base. Entry-level buyers have fewer options in a housing market starved for affordable supply. LGI fills that gap. The risk is that entry-level buyers are the most rate-sensitive cohort in housing. If mortgage payments rise, this customer disappears first.
