The Big Picture

SunCoke handles the unglamorous middle of that chain. It makes coke, moves bulk materials, processes slag, and manages scrap for integrated steel mills. Introducing SXC

SunCoke Energy is the largest independent producer of metallurgical coke in the United States. It operates through two segments: Domestic Coke and Industrial Services. The Domestic Coke segment runs cokemaking facilities that supply blast furnace and foundry coke to integrated steel producers. The Industrial Services segment handles material handling, scrap processing, slag recovery, logistics, and environmental services for steel mills and industrial customers.

The edge is the installed base. SunCoke owns and operates its cokemaking facilities and logistics terminals. Those plants are expensive, permitted, and difficult to replicate. Once a steel producer contracts for coke supply, switching costs are high. The Industrial Services business layers on top of that relationship. SunCoke is inside the mill gates handling scrap, slag, and transportation. That is embedded infrastructure.

The company also exports coke and provides mixing and handling services to coal, power, and utility customers. The diversified service revenue smooths out the cyclicality of pure coke production. You own a company that sells a critical input and then gets paid to handle the byproducts of making steel.
